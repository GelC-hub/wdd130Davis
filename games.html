Video Game Ratings Explained

Video games use a rating system to show the recommended age group and the type of content the game includes. Here are the most common rating symbols and what they mean in simple terms.

E — Everyone

Suitable for all ages. Contains very mild or cartoon-style action.

E10+ — Everyone 10+

May include mild violence or slightly scary themes. Good for most older kids.

T — Teen

Contains moderate violence, mild language, or suggestive content. Best for teens and above.

M — Mature 17+

Stronger content such as intense violence, strong language, or dark themes. Intended for mature players.

A — Adults Only

Contains adult-level material. Rarely used and not recommended for general audiences.
